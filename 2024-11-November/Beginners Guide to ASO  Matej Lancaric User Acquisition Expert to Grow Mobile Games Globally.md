App Store Optimization (ASO) is the ongoing process of maneuvering your way to the top of the search results of different app stores. The higher you show up, the more people will see your app, trust it, and install it, too.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F293d5e2c-592c-4eb5-8117-09e15d8b4b29_1600x1044.png)

[Source: Splitmetrics](https://splitmetrics.com/blog/what-is-app-store-optimization-in-2022/)

**It‚Äôs like SEO but for app stores.** Mastering ASO will be even more important for two reasons. The marketplace is becoming increasingly saturated and competitive, so the more ‚Äúfree UA‚Äù you can get, the better. Also, a beautifully optimized app store listing will help your paid campaigns tremendously. You have to treat it like your golden landing page!

Also, more third-party app stores will begin to appear. It‚Äôs not only the Apple App Store and Google Play anymore (more on this later).

Anyway, let‚Äôs start with the basics.

Do I really need to pay attention to ASO?
-----------------------------------------

Your app listing is your home base. Look at it as if you were running a restaurant. Even if you served the most f\*cking delicious kebabs on the planet, no one is going to want to step foot into your store if your signs are falling apart, you have terrible Google reviews, and if it looks like your windows haven‚Äôt been cleaned in decades.

Every successful app has a great app store page. No exceptions.

ASO gives you the opportunity to be more discoverable. [Google](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/_qs/documents/331/how-users-discover-use-apps-google-research.pdf) says 48% of people discover new apps by browsing app stores. 34% install apps recommended to them in the app store.

But, as we said before, it‚Äôs an ongoing process. You don‚Äôt build a restaurant, just open it, and let it be. You need to clean it up, update the facilities, etc. Maintenance and upkeep are key.

**So yes. You need to pay attention to ASO.**

Is it the only thing you need to pay attention to? Absolutely no.

Is ASO going to save your game or app and help you get millions of organic installs? Most probably, no.

How app stores work
-------------------

Every app store has its own criteria of how to rank each app. While each one carries its own weight and has its small differences, the main elements are the same.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3564d62a-6604-42a5-9f68-b5aa5ea436be_1600x1458.png)

[Source: Semrush](https://www.semrush.com/blog/app-store-optimization/)

**App name or title:** what is your app called?

**Keywords:** a list of words that describe your app and what it does. The more relevant keywords you have throughout your app store listing, the more context it gives to the app store to categorize what you do and who you serve.

**Downloads:** the number of installs and how recent they are

**Reviews and ratings:** the higher the better

**Updates:** are you regularly fixing bugs and adding content to your app

Every app store gives each of these rankings a different weight.¬† For example, Apple‚Äôs App Store lets you input your own keywords. However, you will be penalized if you use too many duplicate keywords.

iOS App Store vs Google Play Store: what‚Äôs the difference?
----------------------------------------------------------

Here‚Äôs a quick side-by-side comparison of the ranking factors. The main difference between the two is that the ***Apple App Store has a keyword field*** where you can manually enter the keywords you‚Äôd like to target. You‚Äôre able to enter as many keywords as you want, but with a 100-character limit. Keywords are separated by commas and have no spaces.

Here are the main things you‚Äôll have to pay attention to on the two app marketplaces:

As of 2024, keywords still play a huge part in ASO. But user engagement and app quality metrics like stability, functionality, usability, and overall user experience are all going to be weighted more heavily in the future.

How to conduct keyword research
-------------------------------

Finding the best keywords needs to have three things. **High relevance, decent traffic, low competition.**

Obviously, more relevant keywords will target audiences that are more likely to install your app. BUT the most relevant keywords may also be the most competitive ‚Äì so your chances of ranking for your category are low, especially if you‚Äôre just starting from scratch. You need to start with high relevance, low competition (and maximum traffic). Then you can build your way up once your app gets bigger and you are able to attract more users.

Let‚Äôs say you have a Meditation app. You‚Äôre going to struggle to rank for the keyword ‚Äúmeditation,‚Äù but might have a higher chance of ranking for ‚Äòmeditation coach‚Äô or ‚Äòmeditation & healing‚Äô or ‚Äòmeditation for kids.‚Äô

Here‚Äôs how to start.

**Step 1: Identify your CORE keywords**

The first step is to establish your core pillar keywords that effectively describe your app. Remember to not duplicate any words and only choose relevant variations of them. Do a massive brain dump using the following categories as a guide. We can trim these down later.

* Core functionality and features
* App categories
* Art style
* Target audience and their interests
* Unique selling proposition (USP)
* Competitor‚Äôs keywords (more on this later)
* Popular trends or buzzwords related to your niche
* Branded keywords (your company, developer, app name)

**Put them all in a spreadsheet with the following columns: keyword, volume, competition, relevance.** You can fill in the data as we go.

**Step 2: Localize and focus on geo**

Now you have to plug in your keywords into your software like AppTweak, Sensor Tower, Mobile Action, Semrush, App Radar, or SplitMetrics. You need to pull the numbers to see what is achievable.

In your keyword research or ASO software, identify which keywords have high traffic and low competition based on your list of keywords above. Your software can also help generate new ideas based on your list, too. But this time, focus on your target geo. US-based keyword results will be very different from Australian, even if the primary language is English.

Getting specific is key. Understanding the local lingo or culture of your target location will help you find valuable and less-competitive keywords. If you aren‚Äôt familiar with the region you‚Äôre targeting, run it by a local expert to make sure you‚Äôre not missing the mark (or get canceled for being insensitive).

**Step 3: Check your current rankings + Apple Search Ads data + AI**

If your app has been listed on app marketplaces, you should have some data around what you‚Äôre showing up for. Even if it isn‚Äôt much, there may be a few hidden gems in there that you can build on, instead of starting from complete scratch.

A good place to start is to dig into your [Apple analytics data](https://developer.apple.com/app-store-connect/analytics/). You can dig into your app store performance and even dive into what search terms and websites drove your installs. It‚Äôll also give you data around conversion rates and how your metadata is affecting your install rates.

**You can also use Apple Search Ads data.**

1. Open up Apple Search Ads and create a campaign
2. Find your app, and select ‚ÄúSearch Results‚Äù and the region you‚Äôre targeting
3. Click ‚Äòcontinue‚Äô and put a random bid amount in ‚Äòad group settings‚Äô (you‚Äôre not using any real money)
4. Under ‚Äòkeywords‚Äô select ‚Äúadd keywords to an Ad Group.‚Äù
5. Look at all the keywords that Apple recommends. The popularity rating should help give a good idea on volume.

**Use Google Keyword Planner**. While this won‚Äôt give you direct mobile marketplace data, it‚Äôs a good way to generate new ideas.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe3902d57-55e1-4aa4-a6ff-63f668ad58da_3008x1354.png)

1. [Open Google Ads](https://ads.google.com/home/)
2. Under tools, choose Keyword Planner
3. Click Discover New Keywords and input all your target keywords
4. Under keyword ideas to generate new keywords
5. Filter based on geography, competition, and device.

Your ASO software should also provide some additional keyword ideas. Again, identify keywords that have a higher search volume, but low competition while remaining relevant to your category. Each ASO software has a different ranking score system, but you should be able to adjust your filters accordingly.

**Step 4: Analyze your competition**

Write down 5-10 competitors that are performing well in your niche. You can conduct ASO research by plugging their names into your software. Typically, they‚Äôll give you a rundown of what they‚Äôre ranking for, what they‚Äôre targeting, and how many installs they‚Äôre generating. This should not only help you ideate new keyword ideas, but set your ASO KPIs as well. Take all the best keyword ideas and add them to your master list.

* Some software can help you identify keywords you‚Äôre ranking for that your competitors aren‚Äôt. So you can double down on these.
* Your competitors may rank for uncontested, but highly profitable keywords. You can be the first to compete with them.
* Some keywords you planned on targeting may be unreachable due to competition. Don‚Äôt throw them away entirely, take note of them, and plan to target them in the long-term.

**Step 5: Refine your targeting**

Now that you have a long-ass list of keywords to target, it‚Äôs time to narrow down your focus on the ones that‚Äôll make an impact on your ASO. If you‚Äôre using an ASO or keyword management software, start by filtering out keywords that are high competition, or extremely low volume, or not relevant at all. Focus on the biggest and most attainable opportunities, first.

How to use keywords to optimize your listing
--------------------------------------------

Okay now you got the biggest step out of the way. Here‚Äôs how to actually start optimizing your app listing.

**App name (title) and subtitle**

This is the first thing users will see in the app store search. Titles and app names can be 30 characters long, so you need to choose wisely. Obviously put your app name in the front. If you have more space, try to fit in one of your target keywords in a natural way.

You can use one of the following combos:

* Brand name + single keyword (Google Photos, YouTube Music, Keepsake Frames)
* Brand name: keyword and phrases (Headspace: Sleep & Meditation, Tinder: Chat, Dating & Friends)
* Condense words and phrases (WeChat, Dropbox)
* Combination of all of them (SwingVision: Tennis Pickleball)

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F39ea6ec9-7c3f-4543-88be-47b13588a4bc_774x1600.jpeg)

This should be an ongoing test. So don‚Äôt worry if you don‚Äôt initially get the results you want ‚Äì you‚Äôll need to monitor it as you continue to optimize your listing.

***You can also add a subtitle*** in the Apple App Store up to 30 characters. This is the second biggest ASO ranking factor so don‚Äôt ignore it. Choose a tagline with your target keyword or a call-to-action.

**Keyword field (Apple App Store only)**

Write up to 100 characters to describe your app. This is not public, so enter all your main keywords and separate them with commas. Commas do not count as a character, so do not add spaces after commas, either. It‚Äôs best to add a mix of highly competitive and less competitive keywords as long as they are relevant to your app.

**Titles of in-app-purchases**

Although this carries less wait, every in-app purchase is crawled by app stores. So you can sprinkle in keywords here.

**Don‚Äôt use duplicate keywords on the App Store**

While Google Play doesn‚Äôt mind if you repeat keywords on the Play Store, iOS only factors it once. So, any duplicates will be a waste of precious character space.

**Google Play package name**

Publish a custom URL on the Google Play store where you can insert your target keyword. However, this URL can‚Äôt be changed so choose wisely.

**Short description**

45 characters on the App Store and 80 characters on Google Play. The beginning of your copy should be catchy and entice users to expand by clicking ‚Äúsee more.‚Äù

**App description**

Up to 4,000 characters for both app marketplaces. This is where you‚Äôll primarily ***stuff*** your keywords. Be sure to make it actually be an enticing marketing and sales pitch! If it sounds too spammy and unnatural, users will be skeptical and not install your app. The #1 factor in all of this is for your app store listing to build trust! So don‚Äôt forget to include any awards or trust symbols (#1 rated, most installed in category, etc) as well.

Non-keyword ASO tips
--------------------

**Update your app regularly.**

**DUH!** App freshness shows app stores that you‚Äôre regularly improving your app experience. This is a positive indicator of UX and also helps users feel like you are committed to improving your app to fix bugs and better meet their needs. In the ‚ÄúRelease notes‚Äù section of Google Play and ‚ÄúWhat‚Äôs new in this version‚Äù on the App Store, you can incorporate keywords in your updates. Updating once or twice a month should suffice! Too many updates can hinder your user experience and lead to uninstalls.

**Make your landing page beautiful.**

Yes and no! If your design is awesome but conversion sucks, you might have a problem. Improving the visual look and feel is a huge factor in improving your conversion rates. Update your app icon, app screenshots, and app preview or promo videos to be on-brand and fresh.¬† Don‚Äôt make it too complicated, but show a preview of the key benefits of your app. If you‚Äôre an ecommerce app, consider using the first preview card to promote any seasonal promotions like Temu:

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F58c86031-fd87-40e6-bef7-0d7a3eed2a60_916x1600.jpeg)

**Run tests all the time**

Whether you‚Äôre using software to do this or you‚Äôre using tools on the App Store directly, run consistent tests once you‚Äôre driving enough traffic to your listing. I recommend about 500 visitors per variation. Like any other test, you‚Äôll need a significant amount of traffic to give you actionable winners for each test. While you can run tests up to 90 days, a few weeks should be sufficient if you have enough traffic from consistent sources.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9c7f71ef-f1d3-47d4-9984-4fca98211662_2000x990.png)

**Run in-app events**

Kind of like Instagram and Snapchat stories, you can run in-app events that take up the majority of your real estate of your App Store listing. These can be everything from premieres, to in-game events, or crazy promotions to showcase what‚Äôs going on in your app right now. Users can search for in-app events directly, which can also bring additional installs.

Looking forward: more and more app stores
-----------------------------------------

Epic Games sued Google and Apple, arguing that their app stores were monopolistic. The jury agreed, and thus would open the door to more app stores appearing on mobile devices. Samsung Galaxy Store, Amazon App Store, Tencent My App, and Huawei App Market already exist.

While Epic lost to Apple but beat Google in the United States, the EU ruled that Apple and Google need to open up their OS to third-party app stores. Microsoft has communicated their plan to open a mobile gaming storefront for iOS, especially if they can get their merger with Activision Blizzard approved.

As of May 23, 2024, the UK [passed a bill](https://www.videogameschronicle.com/news/uk-passes-bill-which-could-force-apple-to-allow-fortnite-and-an-epic-games-store-app-on-ios/) which could force Apple to allow Fortnite and Epic Games Store to launch on iOS.

### Look out for AI-powered App Store Tools

As of March 2024, Apple has started testing an AI-powered ad product similar to Google‚Äôs Performance Max and Meta‚Äôs Advantage+. Google and Meta are already powering up its AI ad tools, but Apple‚Äôs ramping up how advertisers can advertise within the App Store.

Users will be able to input their desired budget, audience, countries, and cost-per-action for two ad formats: ‚Äúyou may also like‚Äù and ‚Äútoday‚Äù tab. These are all signs of increasingly competitive app marketplaces with shrinking organic reach that [all UA managers](http://lancaric.me) should be aware of.
