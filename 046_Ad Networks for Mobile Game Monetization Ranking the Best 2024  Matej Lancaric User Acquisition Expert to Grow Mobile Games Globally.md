## How Do Ad Networks Make Money for Mobile Games?

Ad networks make money for mobile games through models such as Cost Per Mille (CPM), Cost Per Click (CPC), Cost Per Action (CPA), revenue share, in-app advertising, programmatic advertising, premium inventory sales, data monetization and ad mediation platforms.

Here‚Äôs how ad networks make money for mobile games:

### Cost Per Mille (CPM)

Cost Per Mille (CPM) is an advertising model where advertisers pay a fixed rate for every 1,000 impressions that their ads receive. CPM ad networks generate revenue by charging advertisers each time a mobile game displays an ad to a thousand users. Mobile games integrate CPM ads to provide advertisers access to high-traffic gaming audiences. Impressions in mobile games are counted whenever an ad is successfully displayed during gameplay or within in-game menus. Higher user engagement within mobile games results in more impressions which increases revenue generated for CPM ad networks. CPM ad networks utilize algorithms to optimize ad placements to ensure higher visibility for advertisers and maximize impressions for publishers. Advertisers bid for ad placements based on audience demographics and game type, impacting the CPM rate.

### Cost Per Click (CPC)

Cost Per Click (CPC) is an advertising model where advertisers pay each time a user clicks on their ad. CPC ad networks generate revenue by charging advertisers whenever a user clicks on an ad displayed in a mobile game. Mobile games incorporate CPC ads to monetize user interactions by displaying targeted ads during gameplay or in-game interfaces. User engagement with CPC ads directly influences the revenue earned by both the ad network and the mobile game publisher. Advertisers benefit from CPC ads by reaching audiences interested in their products or services to increase the likelihood of conversions. CPC ad networks use algorithms to determine optimal ad placements, aiming to maximize click-through rates and advertiser returns. Advertisers compete for ad space by bidding with higher bids increasing the likelihood of ad visibility within high-traffic games.

### Cost Per Action (CPA)

Cost Per Action (CPA) is an advertising model where advertisers pay only when a user completes a specific action such as signing up or making a purchase. CPA ad networks generate revenue by charging advertisers each time a specified action is completed by a user engaging with a mobile game. Mobile games integrate CPA ads to monetize users based on their actions such as installing an app or registering for a service. Conversions in mobile games are tracked to determine when a user completes the required action that triggers payment. Higher user engagement within mobile games leads to increased conversions, thus raising revenue for CPA ad networks. CPA ad networks employ sophisticated algorithms to strategically place ads where users are more likely to complete actions to optimize both advertiser outcomes and publisher profits. Advertisers bid for ad placements depending on user demographics and expected conversion rates influencing the CPA rate.

### Revenue Share

Revenue Share is an advertising model where publishers and ad networks share a portion of the revenue generated from ad placements. Revenue Share ad networks generate revenue by sharing advertising earnings with mobile game publishers to provide a collaborative monetization model. Mobile games display ads through Revenue Share networks and both the publisher and the ad network earn a percentage of the generated revenue. User engagement in mobile games directly influences the amount of revenue earned as higher interaction with ads leads to increased earnings. Revenue Share ad networks employ algorithms to strategically place ads to optimize both visibility and user engagement. Advertisers benefit from targeted ad placements within mobile games to enhance their reach among engaged users. Publishers including mobile game developers, earn a portion of the revenue to encourage higher-quality ad placements and improved user experience.

### In-App Advertising

In-app advertising is an ad network model where ads are integrated within mobile applications, reaching users while they interact with apps. The primary revenue stream for mobile games through In-App Advertising involves cost-per-click (CPC), cost-per-impression (CPM) and cost-per-action (CPA) models. Ad networks partner with app developers to provide ad inventory in exchange for a share of ad revenue. Game developers place ads at strategically selected touchpoints in mobile games to optimize ad engagement without compromising user experience. Effective ad placement such as rewarded ads, encourages users to interact, generating higher click-through rates (CTR). High CTR directly correlates with increased revenue per ad shown within games. In-app advertising serves advertisers who target specific demographics through data analysis to ensure relevance and user engagement.

Programmatic Advertising is an automated method of buying and selling digital ads using algorithms and real-time bidding. Programmatic ad networks generate revenue by using real-time bidding to sell ad inventory to advertisers targeting mobile game users. Mobile games use programmatic ads to dynamically fill ad spaces based on user data and behavior to optimize ad relevance.
