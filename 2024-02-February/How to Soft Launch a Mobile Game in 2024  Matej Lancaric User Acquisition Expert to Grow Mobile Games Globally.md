What are the Soft launch Stages for Mobile Game?
------------------------------------------------

Soft launch Stages for Mobile Game are as followed:

I like to divide the soft launch into three stages: technical, retention, and monetization. Each stage serves a different purpose, and we measure different KPIs.

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/softlaunch-stages.001.png)

In the [Technical stage](https://lancaric.me/how-to-check-your-games-ua-data/), we mostly focus on data health, crash rate, and tracking. In general, you need to make sure your tracked data that will be used for evaluation and campaign algorithms are correct enough. This is essential for running campaigns effectively.

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/data-check.001.png)

In the Retention stage, we measure D1, D3, and D7 Retention (duh!), FTUE, tutorial completion rate, and much more. Game developers are obsessed with achieving the highest D1 retention numbers, but D1 is not the holy grail. I‚Äôve seen games with D1 retention of around 25% still being able to generate 2 million EUR a month. The magic was behind good D60 retention of 6-8%. Therefore, focusing on the ratio between D1, D3, and D7 is crucial. Joakim described [multiple scenarios here](https://elitegamedevelopers.com/scenarios-for-soft-launch/); definitely¬†check them out.

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/retention-android-US-2023.001.png)

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/ios-retention.001.png)

If you have already launched your app on iOS as well, you can check App Analytics. You can put your app‚Äôs performance into context by comparing it to that of similar apps on the App Store. Compare your results across several key metrics, including conversion rate, day 1, day 7, and day 28 retention rate, crash rate, and average proceeds per paying user to understand your app‚Äôs performance across the customer journey.

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/iOS-Benchmarks.001.png)

In Pre-Launch, you **move gradually** from one test to another, **from Tech to Engagement, Monetization to Soft Launch testing**.

* + Each step has **benchmarks** for **specific KPIs** that help you understand if the game shows potential.
  + If the game is **underperforming,** you have the **opportunity to improve** and continue testing.

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/pre-launch-on-android.001-1.png)

The Google beta program is gaining popularity amongst game developers. Google has done a pretty good job of giving game publishers the tools they need to make their games the best they can be. The benefits of beta testing are obvious: you can test your game with specific groups or open your test to Google Play users and these users **are able to provide private feedback** to developers both **before and after** launch.

At Google Play, they developed different Pre-Launch tracks to help developers test and optimize games before releasing them globally. Each track has its **benefits** and **limitations; you**¬†can easily control them in the **Play Console**. The main idea behind these tracks is to help you test and optimize the game in a **safe environment.**

Available testing options include;

* **Internal testing** is available only to people inside your organization
* **Closed testing** is available to a larger set of testers but is not publicly available
* **Open testing**¬†which will make pre-release versions of your app available on the Google Play store

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/tracks-on-google-play.001.png)

Games in the beta testing program don‚Äôt get reviews on the store, which can be beneficial when something really breaks. A beta testing program is the way to go in a soft launch since you can do the same activities as if your game was live in a [limited amount of GEOs](https://lancaric.me/hybrid-casual-user-acquisition-playbook/).

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/open-beta-testing.001-1.png)

But enough about retention. Let‚Äôs focus on the monetization stage, where the fun starts. Measuring ARPU, payment conversion, and Dx ROAS requires running purchase (ROAS)- oriented campaigns ‚ÄîAEO/VO on Facebook or tCPA/tROAS/AdROAD/[HybridROAS](https://lancaric.me/hybrid-casual-user-acquisition-playbook/) on Google Ads.

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/PI_2024.01.31_Social-Media-use_00_04-1.jpeg)

Let‚Äôs not forget about [Ad monetization](https://lancaric.me/how-to-soft-launch-a-mobile-game-ad-mon-perspective/). In this stage, you should already have cracked the rewarded video ad placement. Now you are playing around with setting up waterfalls and [combining floors with bidders.](https://lancaric.me/ua-creative-learnings-comments-2021/)

If you‚Äôre not familiar with how to manage an ad waterfall or the mediation setup at this stage I would recommend relying solely on Unity ads. Once you‚Äôve set up ads, the North Star KPI you should track at this stage is Impression per DAU. Generally, an IMP/DAU above 4.0 means the Rewarded Video (RV) placements you‚Äôve placed in the game will impact your UA efforts in a meaningful way. If you‚Äôre coming in below this number you either have to change the RV placements, and RV rewards or accept that the Ad revenue will not make up a significant portion of your revenue. If you‚Äôre only using one ad partner at this stage, it is a good general rule of thumb that once you add a mediation platform you‚Äôll increase your ad revenue by 100%. Then with a proper mediation setup, you‚Äôll further increase the ad revenue by another 25%.

How Has the Launch of iOS 14.5 Transformed User Privacy and Attribution for Mobile Games?
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Launch of iOS 14.5 Transformed User Privacy and Attribution for Mobile Games in following ways:

With the launch of iOS 14.5 in April 2021, the ATT era has begun to strengthen user privacy protection, and privacy policies have begun to affect the acquisition of IDFA (accuracy attribution) substantially. LAT started in 2006 has been gradually replaced by ATT (users below iOS 15 account for 6%). For users who upgrade to iOS 14.5 or above after LAT is turned on, the ATT era will continue to have LAT restrictions. Users whose version is still below iOS 14.5 can still obtain IDFA for accurate attribution if they do not turn on LAT. Users who are above iOS 14.5 and do not authorize IDFA need to use SKAN for attribution. However, it has also created significant challenges for marketers who have had to adapt to a completely new way of measuring UA campaigns.

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/ios-14-chalenges.001.png)

Before iOS 14, marketers could measure in-depth and at length to determine user/campaign value. But in Apple‚Äôs SKAdNetwork, data is far less in-depth and at length‚Ä¶You get 64 combinations from 6 bits to map post-install activity and a few days at best of activity data.

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/targeting.002.png)

Most games are driven by revenue conversion schema, with 84% of games utilizing revenue configurations.

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2022/01/unnamed-20.png)

Some game genres have a lower time to first purchase ‚Äì particularly casual games ‚Äì compared to mid-core or 4X strategy. It is absolutely crucial for UA teams to work alongside product/game teams to ensure the game can capture **as many purchases as possible in the first 24 hours**¬†(without jeopardizing the game economy). **This will enable UA managers to scale iOS campaigns.**

How Should User Acquisition Strategies Differ Between PVP and Non-PVP Games?
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

User Acquisition Strategies Differ Between PVP and Non-PVP Games in following ways:

My go-to UA channel in the soft launch was (and still is) Facebook. Given the recent quality fluctuations, I started introducing Google UAC and Unity ads early in the retention stage. How should your strategy differ when you have a PVP game vs a non-PVP game?

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/game-strategy-hybrid.001.png)

You need to keep in mind how many players you need to have statistically significant cohorts to make data-driven decisions. If you have a PVP game, liquidity is the key to your matchmaking, and therefore, the UA team needs to continuously provide a certain number of new users daily to make it work. As we know, if matchmaking doesn‚Äôt work, it can influence the game‚Äôs retention numbers and overall feel.

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/mai-vs-aeo-vs-roas.001.png)

Days when Mobile app install (MAI) optimization was the only way to go are gone. While it can drive good-quality traffic, it won‚Äôt be sufficient for retention and monetization. When you enter the retention stage, sit down with a data team to identify events in the game (tutorial completion, level 10 achieved, 5 missions played, connect Facebook or share invite) that suggest player engagement and higher quality. Then, implement and use these events for optimization in [App Event Optimisation (AEO) campaigns](https://lancaric.me/user-acquisition-beginners-mobile-ua/). Finally, as mentioned before, running AEO Purchase campaigns in the monetization stage will provide the necessary p(l)ayers to evaluate the monetization KPIs of your game.

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/targeting.001.png)

Starting with broad campaigns using regular Facebook campaigns or Advantage+ App campaigns, the targeting will be continuously narrowed down to individual interests or lookalikes later down the road, depending on the results.

You can Effectively Test and Optimize Creative Strategies for Mobile Game User Acquisition by following ways:

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/creative-framework.001.png)

How Should You Diversify Your User Acquisition Portfolio Across Android and iOS?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

You can Diversify Your User Acquisition Portfolio Across Android and iOS in following ways:

Diversifying the UA portfolio is **one of the most important parts** of the marketing activities. In the retention phase, you should think about running at least two channels to benchmark them against each other. Which channels? It depends on your genre, but for example, if you have a merge game. I would go with **Facebook and Unity.**

How about iOS? When should it be added? Honestly, I do it after a few months of testing and running UA on Android. **After you get the initial data on Android, you improve the game, and then you add iOS.**

Ideally just before starting the monetisation phase. So you can configure your conversion schema and start the first tests.

In the monetization stage, different channels yield different LTV curves. Understanding this before the global launch is crucial for setting up the global launch strategy.

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/android-index.001.png)

![](https://lancaric.me/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/ios-index.001.png)

While Google Ads is an important part of Android UA activities, for iOS, it sucks balls but depends if you have unlocked tROAS optimization for iOS it can be game-changing for you. On the other hand, Facebook could work well for iOS but not for Android. Be sure to test multiple ad networks before going global. If your soft launch budget doesn‚Äôt allow it, run Facebook, Google, and Unity as **minimum viable UA portfolio.**
