Managing Allow list and Source Level Bidding for Successful ROAS Campaigns
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

It is a necessary evil to chew through the CPI campaigns, but the ROAS campaigns are worth the pain. You can ease up the pain in two ways.

Launch an¬†**allow list**¬†campaign (speak to your account manager to get best performing subids for your allowlist) or¬†**source level bidding**(speak to your AM to get these bids)

Once you get the xlsx file with bids, country codes, and source IDs from your Unity rep, upload it to your campaign.

Everything looked at first sight according to the Unity template. Still, after importing the file in the Source bids section you got an error message ‚ÄúUploaded document does not contain ‚ÄúSource bids‚Äù sheet.‚Äù don‚Äôt panic.

1. Just go on the campaign page, navigate to the Source bids section, then click Export template.
2. After exporting the template, copy-paste the bids, country code, and source IDs into the downloaded template file.
3. Then click Import to upload your modified spreadsheet.
4. Magic happens the Source bids section will display a list view of the countries you are targeting, and the bid amounts imported from your spreadsheet.

Spent few hours to figure this out.. Enjoy!

What are the Challenges for User Acquisition Operations Q3 ‚Äì 2022?
------------------------------------------------------------------

Privacy and Data Regulations: Stricter privacy laws (e.g., GDPR, CCPA) and changes in data collection practices (e.g., Apple‚Äôs App Tracking Transparency) have made user targeting more challenging, reducing visibility into user behavior and limiting advertisers‚Äô ability to personalize ads effectively.

Increased Advertising Costs: The competition for ad space has continued to increase, particularly on popular platforms like Facebook, Google, and TikTok, leading to rising costs per acquisition (CPA) and requiring UA teams to optimize budgets more efficiently.

Platform Changes and Ad Attribution: Major updates by platforms like Meta, Google, and Apple have caused shifts in how campaigns are tracked and attributed. Accurate measurement of campaign performance has become harder due to limited attribution windows, delayed data, and incomplete conversions tracking.

Economic Uncertainty: With economic uncertainty, potential customers may be more cautious with discretionary spending, making it harder to convert users and impacting the overall return on investment (ROI) for marketing campaigns.

Creative Fatigue: Users are bombarded with ads, leading to creative fatigue. Regularly refreshing creatives while maintaining effectiveness is challenging, particularly with increased costs for content production and influencer partnerships.

Limited Access to First-Party Data: Due to privacy concerns, first-party data is becoming increasingly critical. However, many companies still struggle to gather and leverage this data effectively for user acquisition strategies.

Channel Diversification: Diversifying user acquisition channels has become crucial to spread risk and capture audiences across different platforms. However, it presents challenges in allocating budget effectively, managing multiple platforms, and ensuring consistent messaging.

Algorithm Dependency: Increased reliance on machine learning algorithms for ad targeting by platforms such as Meta and Google means marketers have less manual control over targeting, and success depends on providing enough conversion data to train algorithms effectively.
