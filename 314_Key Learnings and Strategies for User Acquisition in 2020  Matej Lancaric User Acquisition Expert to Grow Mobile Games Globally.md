The evolving landscape of user acquisition (UA) in mobile gaming highlights critical strategies and insights that emerged in 2020. Soft launches were emphasized as a pivotal phase for refining both marketing and game strategies, setting the foundation for long-term success. Google UAC outperformed Facebook in several cases, although Facebook‚Äôs Automated App Ads (AAA) campaigns introduced new opportunities when paired with regular campaigns for a balanced approach. Additionally, Snapchat and TikTok proved to be valuable channels for hypercasual games, delivering high-quality results at significantly lower CPIs.

Creatives remain the cornerstone of UA, with trends, memes, and impactful first seconds playing a vital role in engagement and retention. While video dominates as the primary creative format, static images can outperform in specific genres, and playables yield better results on ad networks rather than Facebook. Optimizing campaigns using proxy events, such as ‚ÄúFree trial + tutorial completed,‚Äù has shown to reduce CPIs while maintaining audience quality. Tailoring strategies to monetization models, such as MAI for ad-monetized games, further enhances campaign effectiveness.

Despite the shift to virtual formats, webinars often lack actionable value, making background checks on organizers essential. Similarly, meetings should be streamlined to avoid inefficiencies. These learnings underscore the importance of innovation, adaptability, and strategic diversification in navigating the dynamic UA ecosystem for mobile gaming.

Thinking ‚Äúoutside of box‚Äù is just not enough nowadays. You have to leverage the **hype & memes** or anything that is trending to maximise the creative performance. Memes can be work very well, you just need to find those that match to your game. ## Static Images: A Simple Yet Effective Creative Format

**Static images** ‚Äì Easy to make, stellar results! Don‚Äôt forget to include static images to your creative mix. Even though video format is the king, static images just cant be overlooked. Probably wont work better than videos for most games, but for some games/genres work like a charm.

## Maximizing Creative Impact: The Importance of First Impressions

**First 1-3 seconds** of creative are really important. I know you hear this from everywhere, but I saw a huge impact in retention on the creative level. Talking about D1 40% vs D1 60% just setting the expectations in a different way.

## Playables on Facebook: A Better Fit for Ad Networks

Playables on FB ‚Äì don‚Äôt work that much. Run them on your ad networks to get better results!

## Optimizing MAI Campaigns for Ad-Monetized Games

MAI optimalisation works only for Ad monetised games. You cant expect IAP ROAS from these campaigns, only Ad ROAS. Using different optimisations depend on your game‚Äôs monetisation model. You can run MAI, Purchase and Value optimisations simultaneously, but you need to **evaluate them differently**. Keep that in mind.

## The Benefits of Testing Proxy Events in Campaign Optimization

Testing different events for optimising campaigns proved to be beneficial. Try to analyse proxy events to purchase. For example running campaigns optimised for ‚ÄúFree trial + tutorial completed‚Äù or ‚Äúlevel 20 achieved‚Äù as events will give you different audience. You will probably see **lower CPIs when compared to purchase/value campaigns**, but similar quality.
